# Skifte Visual Direction

## The Reference Feeling
- Bobo Choses: Big type, editorial, storytelling, warm but not cute
- Primary.com: Bold color blocks, zero filler, every element earns its place
- Maisonette: Generous white space, typography does the work, premium but approachable
- Gelato La Boca: Small brand energy done beautifully — handcrafted, artisanal, warm
- Wade & Leta: Type as art, confident, breathing room, expressive

## What Creates Personality (NOT emoji)
1. **Lilita One at large sizes** — this font IS the personality. Use it at 3xl, 4xl, 5xl. Let it breathe.
2. **Color blocks** — terracotta section, cream section, forest section. Bold blocks of color.
3. **Real photos** — offset, slightly imperfect grid. Not centered stock photos.
4. **Gaegu for body** — handwritten feel gives warmth without decoration
5. **Motion** — things that move naturally, not flashy
6. **White space** — premium brands breathe. Don't fill every gap.

## No Emojis (except functional ones)
- NO emojis in headings
- NO emojis as bullet points
- NO emojis as decorative accents (🌿, ✨, etc.)
- YES emojis only where functional: category icons in Browse, item stage icons in First Year Map

## Color Usage
- Cream (#FAF5EF): hero backgrounds, breathing space
- Terracotta (#C96A3A): CTA sections, accents, active states
- Forest (#3A6349): secondary accents, trust signals
- Sunshine (#F5C842): Baby Stage Guide section only
- White: card backgrounds
- Ink (#3B1F0E): all text — warm brown, never black

## Typography Scale
- Hero headline: text-5xl to text-7xl, Lilita One, ink
- Section headings: text-3xl to text-4xl, Lilita One, ink or white
- Card titles: text-lg to text-xl, Lilita One
- Body: text-sm to text-base, Gaegu, ink/60
- Labels: text-xs, Gaegu, semibold

## Layout Principles
- Cards: rounded-2xl, cream background, subtle border
- Sections: alternate cream/white/terracotta — never same color twice in a row
- Buttons: rounded-full, Lilita One, generous padding
- Images: rounded-2xl, slight offset/stagger in grids

## The Feeling Test
Before publishing any screen, ask: 
"Does this look like something a mom would screenshot and send to her friends?"
If yes — ship it. If no — it needs more work.
