# Skifte Brand Voice & Feeling

## The Core Feeling
Every screen should make a mom feel like she found her people.
Not exclusive. Not corporate. Not a marketplace.
Welcoming. Real. Warm. Like a text from a friend who's been there.

## How We Write

**DO:**
- Write like a real mom talking to another real mom
- Be direct, warm, slightly informal
- Use "you" and "your neighborhood" — make it personal
- Acknowledge the chaos and beauty of motherhood
- Make every action feel like joining something, not using something

**DON'T:**
- Corporate speak ("seamless experience", "streamlined process")
- Cold instructions ("Enter your information")
- Hollow enthusiasm ("Amazing!" "Great!")
- Marketing fluff ("Discover the difference")

## Screen-by-Screen Voice

### Onboarding
- NOT: "Create your profile"
- YES: "Hey! We're so glad you're here. 🌿"
- NOT: "Enter your neighborhood"
- YES: "What neighborhood are you in? This helps other moms nearby find you."
- NOT: "Submit"
- YES: "Join the neighborhood →"

### First Year Map
Each stage tip should sound like a friend who's been there:
- "You'll be amazed how fast they outgrow 3-6M. List it now — another mom nearby is probably looking for exactly this."
- "Crawling means EVERYTHING is on the floor. Time to swap those floor toys up."
- "First shoes are a big deal. And they'll outgrow them in 6 weeks. Skifte was made for this."

### Trust & Safety
- NOT: "Our platform maintains community standards"
- YES: "This is a safe space. We keep it that way together."
- NOT: "User verification protocols"
- YES: "Every mom here is real. We make sure of it."

### Empty States
- NOT: "No items found"
- YES: "Your neighborhood is just getting started. Be the first mom to list something — someone nearby will thank you. 🌿"

### Circles
- NOT: "Join a community group"
- YES: "Find your people. There's a circle for every kind of mom."

### Bottom Nav Labels
- Home → Home
- Browse → Browse  
- Add → Share (because you're sharing, not selling)
- Circles → Community
- Profile → You

## The Promise
Skifte isn't a marketplace. It's a neighborhood.
You don't just get stuff here. You find your people.
